Jenna Ortega Wears All-White for Finestkind Premiere

June 2024 · 2 minute read

Jenna Ortega knows how to make her mark on the red carpet.

The actress, 21, made a grand entrance at the premiere of her latest film, Finestkind. Ortega attended Tuesday night's event at the Pacific Design Center in West Hollywood, California, dressed in a head-turning structured white ADEAM Floral Organza Corset Jacket.

The look tied in perfectly with the light blue and white backdrop for the film's debut.

Keeping with the theme of the dress’ delicate design, the Wednesday star wore her hair styled with wispy bangs as the rest of it was pulled back and away from her face. 

Her bold, but sophisticated jewelry featured silver drop earrings, a single necklace, along with several rings and a bracelet. Her nails were manicured with an off-white tone. She completed her premiere look wearing closed-toe pumps.

The Paramount+ film "tells the story of two brothers, raised in different worlds, who are reunited as adults over a fateful summer" and is set in the backdrop of commercial fishing, according to a synopsis. The brothers — played by Ben Foster and Toby Wallace — are forced to strike a deal with a violent Boston crime gang, and Ortega's character is caught in the middle.

The project was written and directed by Brian Helgeland and also features Tommy Lee Jones, Ismael Cruz Cordova, Aaron Stanford, Scotty Tovar, Tim Daly, Lolita Davidovich and Clayne Crawford.

Ortega's red carpet appearance is a testament to how she is not afraid to experiment with her movie roles or fashion. Earlier this year, she opened up to Vogue about embracing a goth style while filming Wednesday.

"I've always respected goth culture. I've always had a darker sense of humor, but I never felt like I was able to express that. Now I feel like I can embrace those qualities in myself," she said.

Ortega played Wednesday Addams in the hit Netflix series and told the outlet that the role also inspired a hair change.

"Up until then I was really, really attached to my long hair. Changing it encouraged me to be more adventurous; to experiment and be more creative in that way," she told Vogue.
