90s star, 48, looks unrecognizable in rare photos with husband decades after ditching Hollywood for

August 2024 · 3 minute read

A TOP '90s TV star has looked completely unrecognizable as she stepped out to celebrate her husband's birthday.

The actress, now 48, was famed for the NBC TV series My Two Dads and the ABC sitcom Step By Step.

Staci Keanan, who looked adorable with her long blond hair and innocent smile during her '90s fame, made her TV debut back in 1987.

She starred in the miniseries I'll Take Manhattan with Julianne Moore before moving to Los Angeles to film My Two Dads, where she played an orphan.

After Step By Step, where she played Dana Foster, Staci took a break for studies before her final silver screen appearance in the 2010 flick Death and Cremation.

Yet the star swiftly shunned TV fame for a hugely high-profile job.

She attended Southwestern Law School in 2013 and now works as a Deputy District Attorney at the Los Angeles County District Attorney’s Office.

She is also an Adjunct Associate Professor of Law.

The swulaw.edu website summarizes Staci's achievements and states: "Since joining LADA in 2016, she has prosecuted a broad range of matters, including domestic violence, sex crimes, gang crimes, and homicide, from the charge evaluation stage through jury trial.

"She has participated in Project LEAD (a LADA program whereby prosecutors and other LADA staff teach a school-year-long program to a fifth-grade class about the criminal justice system) as a classroom instructor."

Staci, who married actor husband Guy Birtwhistle in 2017, has also re-vamped her look.

Gone are her lengthy locks from her sitcom days - now she rocks a short, layered hairstyle that brushes her shoulders.

She has also lightened her locks to an ash-blond shade and dons a pair of glasses.

Staci was seen parting with pals as she celebrated Guy's birthday in an elegant lilac dress.

The TV personality also rang in 2023 in a flowing black dress and sophisticated hat.

ALL CHANGE

Staci has also changed her name throughout her career.

She was born Anastasia Sagorsky before changing her moniker to Staci Love.

The star then adopted Staci Keanan as her stage name.

It seemed the actress was always going to shun the spotlight.

Back in 1987 at age 12, she told People: “I put in an effort not to be Hollywood.

"I guess when kids or anybody else get conceited, they get an overbuilt self-image, so you have to think about it and not let it happen."
