Walid Sharks Ethnicity: Walid Sharks, the influencer turned boxer, hails from Syria.

He was born into a Middle Eastern Arabic family and had his beginnings in Baghdad, Iraq. What’s truly fascinating about his journey is the remarkable transition from social media influence to the world of professional boxing.

In 2021, he made a significant mark with a highly anticipated match, capturing the attention and admiration of fans, and his fan base continues to grow steadily.

Walid, born on April 15, 2004, is a budding talent who is currently 17 years old, making his mark as a rising star.

Boxer Walid Sharks is part of the star-studded lineup of the Misfits Boxing Prime Card Event.

Following seven years of dedicated boxing training, he reached a significant milestone by winning his first fight on April 16, 2018, which he described as the most incredible and super day of his life.

Notably, he holds a record of 2 wins and 1 loss, and he goes by the nickname “Sharks” in the world of boxing.

Walid Sharks: Ethnicity and background

Walid Sharks hails from Baghdad, Iraq, making him an Iraqi national who follows the Islamic faith.

His family background is quite diverse; his father is of Kuwaiti descent, while his mother is Iraqi. Raised under the influence of both parents, he has a rich Arabic heritage.

Born in 2004, Walid recently celebrated his 19th birthday in April. He has two sisters named Noor and Banen.

Before venturing into the world of boxing, Walid built a name for himself as a model and gained recognition as a TikTok and Instagram influencer.

His financial success is evident, with an estimated net worth ranging between $450,000 and $550,000.

His social media presence is substantial, boasting an impressive following of approximately 443,000 people on Instagram.

Walid vs. Salem: The iconic match unfolds

The YouTube channel “Social Knockout” is well-known for uploading videos related to boxing events.

Walid Sharks, with his strong interest in boxing, decided to participate in one of these knockout matches.

On July 30, an epic showdown took place, featuring Walid Sharks as one of the contenders.

His opponent for the match was Adam Saleh, who, like Walid, is not only a skilled boxer but also a prominent YouTuber.

The intense battle went on for three rounds, and when the dust settled, the match ended in a draw. This outcome left many viewers disheartened, as they were convinced that Walid was the clear winner.

However, the draw had an unexpected and positive consequence.

This viral moment led to a significant increase in his fan following across all his social media platforms.
