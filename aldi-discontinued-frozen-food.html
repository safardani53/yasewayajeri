Customers beg Aldi to bring back discontinued product as store 'shrugs' at possibility of a comeback

April 2024 · 2 minute read

It was all fun and games for Aldi shoppers until they couldn’t find their favorite frozen meal.  

People have taken to social media to beg Aldi to return the popular dish. The discount grocery store had one emoji to say to them.

Aldi has a history of discontinuing or perhaps selling out fan favorite snacks and frozen meals, and its devoted customers won’t stand for it. 

The rolls in question are Bremer’s Cheese Lasagna Rolls.

One shopper was left baffled when she couldn’t find them.

She took to X, formerly known as Twitter, to ask, “Where are my rolls???”

SHOPPERS TAKE TO SOCIAL FOR ANSWERS

Aldi didn’t bite on her urgency and responded, "No return date yet," accompanied by a person shrugging emoji.  

It’s unclear when the supermarket stopped selling the microwaveable cheesy goodness, but shoppers have wondered what happened since early this year. 

Another social user posted on X in March 2023 that she would “sell my boyfriend for one single corn chip… just to have the Bremer lasagna rolls from Aldi's again.” 

And she said she doesn’t even like corn chips. 

Most read in Money

WHAT’S SO SPECIAL ABOUT THESE LASAGNA ROLLS? 

A box of Bremer Cheese Lasagna Rolls contained 12 rolls for the price of $5.99.

That came out to 50 cents per serving. 

Each roll contained ricotta, Parmesan, and Romano cheeses, according to the packaging, and other processed ingredients.

Of course, the lasagna rolls weren’t perfect.

They came without sauce, so you had to make your own.

You could also find the lasagna rolls in a spinach and artichoke flavor.

In this economy, it’s understandable why such an affordable meal would be missed.
