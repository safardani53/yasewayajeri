James David Graham Niven is an English actor, book writer and novelist. He won the Best Actor Oscar for his role as Major Pollock in Separate Tables. Other notable roles include Squadron Leader Peter Carter in A Matter of Life and Death, Phileas Fogg in Around the World in 80 Days, Sir Charles Lytton in The Pink Panther, and James Bond in Casino Royale. Born in London, Niven attended the Heatherdown Preparatory School and Stowe School before earning a place at the Royal Military College, Sandhurst. After Sandhurst, he joined the British Army and was promoted to lieutenant in the Highland Light Infantry. By developing a love for theaters, he found a supporting role in the British film There Goes the Bride. Tired of peacetime troops, he resigned from the commission in 1933, moved to New York, and then to Hollywood.
