July 2024 · 9 minute read

WEST London's most glamorous socialites are back on our screens tonight as the Made in Chelsea gang head to Mallorca.

Four new characters - three ladies and a hunk - are joining the E4 reality show who reportedly fit the "essential criteria of being... rather stylish, sexy and, of course, terribly posh".

While a sprinkling of the original members - including Ollie Locke and Mark-Francis Vandelli - are still part of the cast, most of the people who made the show famous have moved on to do very different things.

While some have continued to establish their reality TV careers, others have opted to step out of the spotlight. 

But one thing is for sure, almost all of them are still flush with cash.

Here we take a look at their varied fates and fortunes.

Agne Motiejunaite

Agne, now 36, was Francis Boulle’s glamorous assistant who found herself caught in a love triangle between the businessman and his best pal Fredrik Ferrier. 

She left after just one series and is now an influencer who shares her travels with her 96.3K followers on social media. 

Agne often post pictures with her famous pals including Jackie Jackson and Gary Lineker’s son George.

She also works as a model and has fronted campaigns for Harley-Davidson motorbikes, a protein shake company called Fightline and Vision Express.

She recently marked her third wedding anniversary with a stunning snap showing off the flowing gown she wore on her big day, but keeps her husband's identity hidden.

Alexandra 'Binky' Felstead

Fan favourite Binky, 32, stayed in the show until 2017 when she opted to leave after falling pregnant with her first child. 

She was part of a trio with Cheska Hull and Ollie Locke, who were always together throughout the early series. 

Binky’s relationships and break-ups with Alex Mytton and JP Patterson played out on the show, but she has since found love with businessman Max Fredrik Darnton, who she married last year.

The couple got engaged after dating for just 19 months and welcomed their first child in June 2021. They had a second wedding in Corfu last month.

Since leaving the show, Binky has launched Mama Being - a fitness app aimed at mums - and had her own show about raising her daughter.

Binky made a cameo in the 20th series of MIC to be part of best pal Ollie’s wedding. She’s thought to be worth £1.4million thanks to the show and her businesses she has set up since. 

Amber Atherton

Savvy businesswoman Amber, 31, stayed on Made in Chelsea for three series while she built up her business My Flash Trash. 

My Flash Trash went on to become a firm favourite with celebs and saw her land a spot on Forbes' 30 under 30 list before she sold it for a reported £2million. 

Since then she worked on a new brand, Zyper, a marketing software startup, and is currently head of strategic communities at Discord.

She recently shared snaps of herself looking cosy with venture capital guru Vishal Lugani on holiday in Capri.

Catherine 'Caggie' Dunlop

Caggie’s return to Chelsea set the scene for Made in Chelsea and started fireworks thanks to her on/off romance with bad boy Spencer Matthews. 

The 32-year-old stayed in the show for three series, despite deciding to move back to New York at the end of series one to get away from Spencer. 

After leaving Made in Chelsea, she moved to Australia to focus on her music career as well as hosting a podcast, Saturn Returns.

We're unlikely to see Caggie on MIC again - she previously admitted: "I don't like reality TV. A little ironic, I know."

Francesca 'Cheska' Hull

Cheska, 37, joined the cast for the first series with best pals Binky and Ollie who attempted to help with her struggling love life.

During her time on the show she had furious clashes with Victoria Baker-Harber before she left in 2014. 

She claims her last two years on the series left her with “anxiety and depression” but did later make a cameo in 2017 to share her pregnancy news with Binky - who was also pregnant. 

Since leaving the show she helped co-found her own PR company Big Smoke PR.

Cheska is now raising her son Charlie in Devon. She also has her own business creating and selling interesting pieces like black resin coasters and jewellery from oyster shells.

Francis Boulle

Dimond-minder Francis, 33, is one of the wealthier stars of the show who has been there since the beginning, dipping in and out. 

He has appeared in Tatlers’ Eligible Young Bachelor guide multiple times thanks to his fortune and family connections. 

Francis, who is worth an eyewatering £10 million, grew up in France and the US and was educated at the prestigious Oratory School with some of his castmates. 

In recent years he has worked for his family’s mining business and diamond brokerage and invested in some tech startups. 

He's finally found love with stunning French fashion model Zoé Gegout, with whom he shares a cocker spaniel called Bill.

Fredrik Ferrier

Fredrik, 33, is the best pal of Francis Boulle and went to the Oratory School with him. 

The model proved popular with the ladies in the first and latter series. 

He mainly grew up in Norway but also spent time in Moscow, Texas, Damascus, Reykjavik and Copenhagen.

Despite being close to Francis the pair fought over Agnes, with neither managing to woo her. 

Fredrik has dipped in and out of the show, last but has been more prominent in recent series - and is a close pal of co-star Sophie Hermann.

Outside of Made in Chelsea he is a model, the Director and Founder of private art gallery GEIST and an aspiring singer-songwriter. 

Funda Önal

Funda, 37, was Spencer Matthew's poorly treated girlfriend in series one of the show and struggled with his feelings for Caggie. 

She was often seen frustrated with her beau and the two eventually ended up splitting. 

Funda is a model and dancer who has appeared on the X Factor and in Harry Potter.

Since leaving the show after the first series she has settled down, married and had a daughter with husband Scott Sullivan.

Gabriella Ellis

Singer, 31, Gabriella was Ollie’s girlfriend during series one and was left heart-broken when he dumped her - saying he wanted to date guys. 

She was shown to be close with his pals Cheska and Binky who attempted to help her get through the breakup. 

Memorably she cast a music video with dancers who all looked very similar to ex Ollie. 

She stayed in the main cast for four series before moving to America to ignite her music career. 

Since leaving Gabriella has rebranded herself as London Ellis - a singer and blogger living 'The London Life', but based in L.A.

Hugo Taylor

Hugo, 36, caused a splash in series one by cheating on girlfriend Millie Mackintosh - who has since become his wife - with her best friend Rosie Fortescue.

The eligible bachelor, who is worth £3 million, was best friends with bad boy Spencer Matthews and did his best to calm Spencer’s wild side. 

After three series he decided to exit the show and has been building up his luxury sunglasses brand Taylor Morris. 

He’s since welcomed two daughters with Millie.

Louise Thompson

Louise, 32, entered the show briefly in series one before she started dating Spencer in series 2.

She was repeatedly cheated on throughout their romance which shattered her confidence. 

But things got better for the student as the series progressed and she even met her fiancee personal trainer Ryan Libby on the show. 

She left the show briefly after the 16th series but returned two later before leaving again in 2020. 

Since her departure, Louise, who is worth £1.2 million, has started up a fitness business called Live Like Louise and a clothing line called Pocket London. 

She is one of the few members of the cast who is a Chelsea native. 

Louise had a horrific experience while giving birth to her and Ryan's son Leo-Hunter, which has left her battling PTSD.

Mark-Francis Vandelli

Mark-Francis, 33, is without a doubt the poshest member of the Made In Chelsea cast and also the longest-serving. 

He is the son of a Russian princess, Diane Boulting-Casserley Vandelli, who was a muse of Yves Saint-Laurent in the 1970s and '80s.

Mark-Francis, who is worth £2 million, often stays above the drama and is often seen hiring staff or talking about items he owns and the correct way to behave. 

He owns a French atelier, Gripoix in Paris, but still makes time to stir the drama on the Channel 4 show. 

Millie Mackintosh

Millie, 33, is the granddaughter of the inventor of Quality Street but won’t inherit the company as it was sold by her family. 

During the first series, she had a huge row with Hugo - which saw her throw a drink in his face after finding out he had cheated. 

She spent five series on the show before opting to focus on her make-up work and modelling.

Millie, who is worth £2 million, had a brief marriage to Professor Green before divorcing and reconciling with Hugo.

Ollie Locke

Ollie, 35, certainly wasn’t wealthy compared to his castmates when he appeared as the guest list manager of Raffles in the opening of the series.

But since then his life has been totally turned around by the series and he’s now worth a cool £6 million. 

In 2020 he married his husband Gareth Locke on Made In Chelsea, and they've since been trying to get pregnant via a surrogate, who sadly miscarried last year. 

Outside of the show he is a model and even penned his own book Laid in Chelsea which is about his sex life. 

Rosie Fortescue

Rosie, 32, graced our screens for 13 series and even boasts attending the same school as future queen Kate Middleton - Downe House. 

Her father is the director of Seymours Art Advisors and is a millionaire. Rosie on her own is worth £1.1 million

She wasn’t one to shy away from controversy and even hooked up with her best friend's boyfriend - which was publicly revealed during the show. 

Since exiting the show she was been working on her blog, Fashion Forte, and has a column in the Evening Standard. 

Outside of this, she has collaborated with designers Covert Cases and Laura Gravestock, and has her own jewellery range named after herself. 

Spencer Matthews

Spencer, 34, is the original rich playboy hailing from a family who own the prestigious Eden Rock Hotel in St Barths.

He’s certainly had a posh upbringing as he attended Eton - where Prince Harry and William went - before going on to get a job as a foreign exchange broker.

Spencer, who is worth £2.9 million, cheated on all of his girlfriends during the course of Made in Chelsea despite being berated and caught several times. 

Outside of his reality TV work, Spencer is a shareholder in his parents' hotel and several estates in Knightsbridge and the South of France.
