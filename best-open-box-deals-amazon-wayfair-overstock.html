The retailers with the best open box deals that can save you $400 - from Amazon to Wayfair

September 2024 · 4 minute read

FROM coupons to discounts and clearance sales to markdowns, there are tons of ways consumers can find low prices.

The challenge is often keeping up with offers, knowing what coupons to clip, and where to shop at what time.

Some retailers have an endless supply of great deals that you can browse every day though.

The only catch is you won't be getting a brand new product.

What are open box deals?

If you see an item marked "open box," that means it's been opened and returned by another consumer.

Returned items are inspected for quality and placed back on shelves but at a lower price.

In most cases, restocked products weren't returned for any defect, but simply because the first buyer wanted another color or model.

So near-pristine products become available for up to 67% off including tablets, furniture, and appliances.

We put together a list of retailers with the best open box inventories so you can start taking advantage of these deals if you aren't already.

Amazon

Amazon Warehouse is home to thousands of returned and relisted products across more than a dozen categories.

Before listing, Amazon tests the functionality and condition of the particular product.

Once the product is on Amazon’s marketplace, you’ll notice four categories describing the condition:

  • Used - Acceptable
  • Used - Good
  • Used - Very good
  • Used - Like new

According to Amazon, some products might be discounted higher than others because of the item condition.

Every Warehouse purchase comes with a 30-day return policy, but no warranty.

The best deals we spotted include an Xbox Series S that costs $499.99 new for $254.69, and a Chromebook that sells for $219 listed at $72.35.

Offers come and go as products get returned, but Amazon never has a shortage of deals available.

One downside is that Warehouse doesn't show the item's list price, so you'll need to do some comparisons to see how much you're saving.

Best Buy

Mostly featuring electronics and appliances, Best Buy offers up to 60% off on some returned products.

You can find great value on accessories, like Samsung and Apple phone cases for under $3.

But the best deals come on the bigger buys.

We found a refrigerator-freezer combo marked down from $989.99 to $692.99 and multiple dishwashers for more than 25% off.

Best Buy has no specific return policy for open box buys, so you'll have 15 days to return it like any other product from their shelves.

Wayfair

If you're looking for discounted furniture, Wayfair's open box deals are a great place to start.

One of the best deals available was a king bed for $202.40 - 67% off its face value.

You can also find dressers for up to 50% off, desks for less than $100, and bedding marked down by 60%.

Wayfair's open box catalog changes quickly and often, so keep an eye on deals you like.

Wayfair does not allow returns on open box products - you can try calling customer service and negotiating, but its policy says no returns.

Overstock

Overstock has similar open box products to Wayfair and generally offers cheaper prices but with fewer options to choose from.

The wholesale retailer labels its returned and repackaged products with "as is".

Overstock's open box webpage is not easily filterable or searchable, so you'll have to comb through carefully to spot the best offers.

Still, after a bit of scouring, we spotted bedding discounted by as much as 50%, a wide array of interior lighting products for 30 to 45% off, and even a black tuxedo for less than $60.

Overstock unfortunately does not offer returns on open box products.
