Vanderpump Rules' Tom Sandoval and Ariana Madix sued for their Fancy AF Cocktails recipe book

May 2024 · 4 minute read

VANDERPUMP Rules' Tom Sandoval and Ariana Madix were sued for their 2019 Fancy AF Cocktails: Drink Recipes from a Couple of Professional Drinkers recipe book.

This new lawsuit comes just a few days after The Sun revealed Tom and Tom Schwartz, 38, are looking into opening their own bar.

According to court documents obtained by The Blast and Us Weekly, Tom and Ariana, 35, are being sued over breach of contract by the book's writer, Alison Baker, who has co-authored seven books with various celebs.

In the lawsuit, Baker explains that she met Ariana through a mutual friend in 2016, which is when they allegedly began discussing writing a cocktail recipe book from the Vanderpump Rules' star's perspective.

Baker then claimed that she and Ariana entered an agreement for services rendered as co-author in exchange for an initial fee of $3,000 and 45 percent of the total advance paid by any publisher who published the book based on the concept.

She alleged that their the agreement stated that Baker would earn 45 percent "actual earned royalties on sales of such book in perpetuity" and co-author credit on the title page.

In the doc, she then claimed that Tom, 37, "forced himself" into their agreement and suggested that he and Ariana published a separate book based on the original concept.

Following Tom's new role, Baker claimed she created a new agreement with the couple, in which they agreed to "share any revenue from this separate endeavor in equal thirds."

Sadly, Tom and Ariana allegedly sent her back a revised version of the agreement because they were not "prepared to contribute the level of work necessary to complete the task of successfully writing and self-publishing a book."

Because of this, the book deal was canceled.

However, according to the lawsuit, the couple then wrote their own book, which Baker learned about in August 2018.

The book – which was released a year ago – allegedly "incorporates many of the proprietary rights" based on the original book concept, resulting in a breach of the first contract.

She further claimed that the book's release was "willful, malicious, and fraudulent" and she is now entitled to damages and attorney’s fees.

She's asking for an accounting of all monies and benefits received or to be received for the sales of the “infringing book,” general and specific damages in an amount to be decided upon at trial, “reasonable” attorney fees, and further relief as the court sees fit.

The Sun has reached out to Ariana and Tom's reps, but failed to hear back at the time of publication.

This lawsuit comes just a few days after a source exclusively told The Sun revealed that Tom and Tom are ready to part ways with their business partner Lisa Vanderpump and open up a bar on their own.

The source said: "They are planning to open a bar by themselves, without Lisa, because they want bigger pieces of the pie."

"They are already looking at real estate in Los Angeles and West Hollywood for their next spot."

The friends have not burned bridges with former boss Lisa, the source assured: "They have not had a falling out from Lisa, they just want to venture out on their own."

Reps for the pals did not immediately respond to The Sun's request for comment.

Unfortunately, TomTom continues to be closed due to the surging Covid-19 cases in LA.
