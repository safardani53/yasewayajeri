Samsung admits to Galaxy S10 screen protector bungle that lets ANYONE unlock your phone and prom

September 2024 · 4 minute read

SAMSUNG owners are being warned about a glitch that hands anyone access to their messages, photos and banking apps.

The South Korean tech titan has admitted that some of its phones can be unlocked using a cheap screen protector sold online.

It follows the shock news, revealed exclusively by The Sun last week, that a Brit Mum had found a simple way to bypass the fingerprint lock on her Samsung.

Lisa Neilson, 34, discovered that anyone could access her Galaxy S10 after she fitted a £2.70 screen protector she got on eBay.

The Mum-of-two bought the gel cover – which was not an official Samsung product – to protect her new phone.

With the screen on, Lisa set up her right thumb print to access the phone but later used her left, which unlocked it.

She found any finger print unlocked the phone.

Other reports have since suggested the Samsung Note 10, released in August, suffers similar security issues.

Samsung said it is investigating the problem and aims to fix it as soon as possible.

The firm is hoping to release a software update that will make its fingerprint sensors more secure.

"We are investigating this issue and will be deploying a software patch soon," a spokesperson told The Sun.

"We encourage any customers with questions or who need support downloading the latest software to contact us directly at 0330 000 0333."

They added that the issue only occurs when the device is being used with third-party protectors.

Speaking to The Sun last week, Lisa, from Castleford, West Yorks, said her screen protector trick could have handed anyone access to her bank details.

"Anyone can access it and could get into the financial apps and transfer funds," she said.

Samsung said people should only use authorised screen protectors.

She got husband Wes, 34, to try and both his thumbs were also able to open the phone through the gel cover.

Mum-of-two Lisa also checked her sister’s Samsung and it was exactly the same when fitted with the case.

Samsung – a brief history

Here's what you need to know...

  • Samsung is a major South Korean company made up of many businesses that operate globally
  • It's known locally as a "chaebol", which means "business conglomerate"
  • It was founded by Lee Byung-chul in 1938 as a trading company
  • But over several decades, it branched out into food processing, insurance, textiles and retail
  • It wasn't until the late 1960s when Samsung entered the electronics industry – for which it's best known in the west today
  • It also launched businesses in construction and shipbuilding in the 1970s
  • Today, Samsung's most important sources of income are its smartphones and computer chips
  • The firm accounts for around a fifth of South Korea's total exports, and roughly 17% of the country's GDP
  • More than 320,000 staff are employed by Samsung globally
  • And in 2017, Samsung turned over the equivalent of £174billion today in revenue
