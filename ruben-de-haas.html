Career

  • De Haas signed an academy contract with the South African provincial union the Free State Cheetahs in July 2017 after representing the United States on age-grade level teams.
  • He played for the Free State U19 side in the 2017 Under-19 Provincial Championship, as well as for university side CUT Ixias in the 2018 Varsity Cup, before making his first-class debut in South African domestic competition for the Free State XV against the Griffons in the 2018 Rugby Challenge.
  • The Free State Cheetahs announced in May 2018 that he had signed a deal with the Cheetahs, the union’s Pro14 squad, until the end of October 2020.
  • De Haas joined Saracens ahead of the 2021-22 season, it was announced in February 2021.
  • De Haas made his debut for the United States as a part of the men’s national under-19 team (High School All-Americans) during their 2016 tour of Ontario, Canada. De Haas led the High School All-Americans as captain.
  • De Haas made his debut in the 2016 World Rugby Under 20 Trophy with the United States men’s national under-20 squad (Junior All-Americans).
  • On April 19, de Haas made his Junior All-American debut as a 65th-minute replacement in a 46-44 defeat to Namibia.
  • On April 23, De Haas made his first start at scrum-half for the Junior All-Americans in their 32-12 triumph against Hong Kong.
  • De Haas also appeared twice for the Junior All-Americans in World Rugby Under 20 Trophy qualifying matches in 2017.
  • On June 13, 2017, he scored one conversion in the Junior All-Americans’ 46-12 loss to Canada.
  • On June 17, 2017, he also scored one conversion and one penalty goal in the Junior All-Americans’ 27-25 win against Canada. De Haas was the Junior All-Americans’ vice-captain.
  • On October 7, 2017, De Haas made his first appearance with the USA Selects, starting at scrum-half in the Selects’ 48-26 loss to Samoa in the 2017 Americas Pacific Challenge. De Haas scored his first try for the Selects on October 15, 2017, appearing as a substitute in the Selects’ 45-26 victory over Canada.
  • De Haas made his USA Eagles debut on February 17, 2018, as a replacement in the Eagles’ 45-13 Americas Rugby Championship win against Chile. De Haas appeared in four matches for the United States at the 2019 Rugby World Cup.
