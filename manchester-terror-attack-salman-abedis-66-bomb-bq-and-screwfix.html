August 2024 · 5 minute read

EVIL Salman Abedi’s bomb was put together for £66 using easy-to-buy items from B&Q and Screwfix.

He carried the device in a £37 Karrimor rucksack bought from Sports Direct in central Manchester.

Nuts, bolts and screws costing £18 from the two DIY stores were packed into the bomb as shrapnel.

And to power its detonation, the 22-year-old maniac may have got a £11 battery from Toolstation.

An assistant at its store in Ancoats, six minutes from Abedi’s rented flat and bomb factory, said she recognised his picture — telling The Sun: “His face looked really familiar.”

Cops are investigating if Abedi made the device or was helped by a bomb maker. Mike McCaul, of the US House Homeland Security Committee, has said the backpack was loaded with the explosive TATP — dubbed the Mother of Satan — and the same as that used in the November 2015 attacks in Paris and the Brussels attack in March 2016.

Expert Hamish de Bretton-Gordon said: “It’s classic Islamic State. The parts can be bought readily.

"Around 5kg to 10kg of TATP would cause the type of blast seen in Manchester.”

Meanwhile it emerged Abedi fled a one-bed flat at midnight to return to Libya weeks before his attack.
Landlord Aimen El Wafi, 38, rented out the pad in Blackley, Manchester, via Gumtree. Abedi paid around £700 in cash to stay for around six weeks before leaving in April.

Libyan-born solicitor Mr El Wafi told friends he believes Abedi’s brother Ismail, 23, may have also stayed at the flat after finding a discarded sleeping bag on the sofa and several pairs of shoes.

Muhammad El Hudari, 39, a business partner of Mr El Wafi said: “Abedi called Aimen to say he needed to leave right now. He left the key with neighbours.

“Aimen told him he hadn’t taken his stuff (sleeping bag and shoes), but Abedi said to dump it as he didn’t want it any more.

“He said he had to get out of here and he was going to travel home to Libya. That was the last Aimen heard from him.”

Mr El Wafi then found “something strange” about how the flat was left.

His friend added: “The curtain had been cut into tiny squares, he found a metal strip in the bathroom and the mains electricity was switched off. There was a rank odour which suggested the windows had been shut for weeks.

“Aimen said to me, ‘This person was doing something in the flat, maybe parties, drugs, alcohol’, but he didn’t think it was bomb making.

“When he saw the news, he was shocked and panicked.

“He went to the police station to tell them what he knew.

“He was in shock, he sat in his car for one or two hours thinking about what he should do before going to the police. He is still in custody and his lawyer says he expects him to be released on Saturday.”

Armed cops stormed the property on Wednesday. Another flat rented by Abedi in Granby Row in the city centre was raided the same day.

It is thought to be where he put the finishing touches to his bomb.

New evidence suggests Abedi could have been radicalised as early as November 2012. In an encrypted BlackBerry messenger conversation, he refers to “kofar sons of b*****s”.

“Kofar” is a derivative of kuffar, an offensive Arabic term used to describe non-Muslims.

It had been thought Abedi started plotting just a year ago.

The transcript, which is all in Arabic, was published online by cousin Abz Forjani. Whoever starts the chat says: “Am going to but my klashinkov in there Ass you will see.”

Abedi then says “F*** those m******f**** kofar sons of b****s”.

Mark Roberts, a GCSE media studies tutor at Burnage Academy for Boys, described ex-pupil Abedi as a “dislikeable, lazy boy”.

He said the suicide bomber showed “no savage violence, no radical tendencies...no signs of a young man that wanted to take life through the most callous and senseless of acts.” Mr Roberts added: “All I can give are banal anecdotes about a dislikeable boy who displayed average laziness, mediocre rudeness and refused to complete his coursework on time.”

British anti-terror cops have now arrived in Tripoli where Abedi’s father and younger brother Hashem, 18, are still in custody.

A spokesman for the Libyan police said: “We are giving all the assistance necessary.

“The father insists he knows ­nothing but the younger son has told us plenty.

“He said he had also been in Manchester last month to help prepare the bomb and he is a member of ISIS.

“We have spoken to the mother and sister as well but they have not been arrested.

“The mother is very upset. She can’t believe what her son has done.”
