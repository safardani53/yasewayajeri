Christian Wood | Career

High School Career

Wood enrolled at Los Alamitos High School during his first year and later shifted to Knight High School in Palmdale, California.

After spending one year at Knight, he transferred to Findlay Prep in Henderson, Nevada.

In his stints in Findlay Prep, he assisted the team in winning 54 consecutive games and an ESPN National High School Invitational championship in 2012.

In the meantime, Wood was rated as the No. 36 overall player in the class of 2013 by Rivals, and he was ranked No. 10 at his position by Scout and No. 71 prospect overall by ESPN.

College Career

Wood attended UNLV university, where he played for the UNLV Runnin’ Rebels men’s basketball team from 2013 to 2015.

He appeared in 30 games with two starts during his freshman year and logged 4.5 points and 3.2 rebounds per game.

He appeared in 33 games during his sophomore season and logged 15.7 points, ten rebounds, and 2.7 blocks per game.

Thus, he earned first-team All-Mountain West honors, and after the end of the season, Wood had announced for the 2005 NBA Draft.

Professional Career

Wood went undrafted in the 2005 NBA Draft and later joined the Houston Rockets for the 2015 NBA Summer League.

Then, in September 2015, he signed with the Philadelphia 76ers. Wood made his debut for the 76ers in October 2015 and logged two rebounds in five minutes off the bench against the Boston Celtics.

After dealing with numerous assignments with the Delaware 87ers of the NBA Development League during his rookie season, 76ers waived him in January 2016.

The Delaware 87ers acquired wood in January 2016. Moreover, he returned to the 76ers in a 10-day deal with the team. After spending three days with the 76ers, the team waived him, and the Delaware 87ers reacquired him.

Interestingly, Wood was called again by the Philadelphia 76ers to sign another 10-day contract.

In April 2016, he signed with the 76ers for the remaining of the season. And in July 2016, he re-appeared with the 76ers for the 2016 NBA Summer League.

Wood signed with the Charlotte Hornets in July 2016, and in November 2016, he debuted for the team against the Indiana Pacers.

Also, he’d multiple assignments with the affiliated team of Hornets, Greensboro Swarm of the NBA Development League.

Later, Wood played with Dallas Mavericks and Phoenix Suns for the 2017 NBA Summer League.

During that eternity, he won the Summer League Championship in Orlando with the Mavericks.

In August 2017, Wood signed with the Fujian Sturgeons of the Chinese Basketball Association, but the team waived him without appearing in a single game.

In the same year, he returned to the Delaware 87ers. He signed with Milwaukee Bucks in August 2018, and after some stints, he was waived by the Bucks in March 2018.
