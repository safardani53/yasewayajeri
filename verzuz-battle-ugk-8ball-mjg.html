Verzuz UGK vs 8Ball &amp; MJG: How can I watch the battle?

July 2024 · 2 minute read

SOME of hip-hop's most noteworthy performers have taken the stage for the online streaming series known as Verzuz.

Created by famed music producers Swizz Beatz and Timbaland, the next acts to take the stage will be rap duo UGK against fellow rap duo, 8Ball and MJG.

How can I watch UGK and 8Ball & MJG's Verzuz battle?

In January 2021, former UGK rapper Bun B revealed that he was planning on making an appearance on an episode of Verzuz.

The Texas musician said on The Donnie Houston Podcast: "I’m doing a Verzuz but it’s not Texas-based."

"It’ll be UGK probably against 8Ball and MJG. Everything’s pushed back because of the Ashanti and Keyshia Cole [delay]."

"They’re still trying to find the right times to do a lot of this stuff, but I would imagine before the summer."

Bun B further added: "It wasn’t about charting artists, or who had the hot record, it was about creating a vibe."

"I think UGK and 8Ball & MJG would’ve worked under those circumstances."

"The fans obviously still wanna see it and we’re ready to do it if called on."

A year-plus after expressing interest in an episode of Verzuz, the battle is coming to fruition.

On May 26, 2022, UGK will face off in a rap battle against Tennesse hip-hop hitmakers, 8Ball and MJG.

The live episode is scheduled to be filmed from Atlanta, Georgia, and will commence on Thursday at 8pm EST/5pm PST.

Music fans can tune into the battle by viewing the live stream from Verzuz's official Instagram page.

For more information, viewers can check out Verzuz's official website.

Who is UGK?

Hailing from Port Arthur, Texas, UGK was formed by Chad Butler and Bernard Freeman - otherwise known as Pimp C and Bun B.

The duo's stage name stands for Underground Kings.

The twosome experienced success throughout the 1990s and the 2000s.

They also appeared on hit singles by Jay-Z, Outkast, TI, and Three 6 Mafia.

UGK remained active until Pimp C's death on December 4, 2007.

Who are 8Ball and MJG?

Memphis-based hip-hop stars 8Ball and MJG met in 1984 and released their debut studio album Comin' Out Hard in 1993.

During their time in the spotlight, the two released projects together as well as singlehandedly.
