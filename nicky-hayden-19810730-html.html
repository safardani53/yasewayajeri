Nicholas Patrick Hayden, nicknamed "The Kentucky Kid", is an American professional motorcyclist who won the 2006 MotoGP World Championship. Hayden started motorcycling as a child. He began his racecar racing career at CMRA before moving to the AMA Supersport Championship and later the AMA Superbike Championship. He won the AMA title in 2002 and was approached by the Repsol Honda team to race them in MotoGP. In his first season, he performed very well, finishing 5th in the championship and winning the rookie of the year award. However, it was followed by a difficult second season in which he finished eighth overall. Hayden then bounced back in the 2005 season by claiming his first Grand Prix victory at Laguna Seca and finishing third in the standings at the end of the season. The following year, 2006, will be the biggest year for Hayden in motor racing as he wins the 2006 MotoGP World Championship, breaking Valentino Rossi's five-year record.
